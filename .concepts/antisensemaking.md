### Antisensemaking

Claim: Instead of just considering sensemaking in the positive, we should also try to develop an understanding of sensemaking in the negative capacity, which examines how we ‘fail to make sense of things’.

This is similar to [[antimemetics]], and would cover subjects like individual blocks, cogsec failures, low-status vortexes, mimetic rivalry between subcultures, abnegation, the dark forest that undescribes itself, NYT-like patterns of institutional failure, all of which yield interesting insights when we make a point to examine them.

I'll also claim that antisensemaking is a key to closing the gap between Stage 3 scenes and Stage 4 institutions, and thus a form of Stage 5 metasystematicity as suggested by Robert Kegan.

_From a conversation with [[Ivan Vendrov]] in November 2025._
